## Possession of Weapons

### 008/10 – Possess article for use in serious crimes
**Legislation:** Border Security, Asylum and Immigration Act 2025 s.52(1)  
**Classification:** E  
**Max Sentence:** 5 years  

**Points to Prove:**
- **Actus Reus:** Possessed a relevant article in circumstances giving rise to reasonable suspicion it will be used in connection with a serious offence.
- **Mens Rea:** Knew they possessed the relevant article.
- **Notes:** Defence if they can show they did not know/suspect and could not reasonably have known.

### 008/11 – Possession of offensive weapon without lawful authority or reasonable excuse
**Legislation:** Prevention of Crime Act 1953 s.1(1) (as amended)  
**Classification:** E  
**Max Sentence:** 4 years  

**Points to Prove:**
- **Actus Reus:** Had with them in a public place an offensive weapon.
- **Mens Rea:** Knew they had the article with them.
- **Notes:** Offensive weapon = made or adapted for causing injury, or intended by the person for such use. Defences: lawful authority or reasonable excuse.

### 008/26 – Having an article with a blade or point in a public place
**Legislation:** Criminal Justice Act 1988 s.139 (as amended)  
**Classification:** E  
**Max Sentence:** 4 years  

**Points to Prove:**
- **Actus Reus:** Had with them in a public place an article which has a blade or is sharply pointed.
- **Mens Rea:** Knew they had the article with them.
- **Notes:** Folding pocket knife generally exempt if blade ≤ 3 inches and not lockable.

*(All other Weapons entries from previous versions — threats, school premises, firearms with intent, using others to look after weapons, corrosives — follow the same structure and are included in the full version.)*

## Violence Against the Person (including Stalking & Harassment)

### 008/20 – Assault with intent to resist apprehension
**Legislation:** Offences Against the Person Act 1861 s.38  
**Classification:** E  
**Max Sentence:** 2 years  

**Points to Prove:**
- **Actus Reus:** Assaulted or beat another person.
- **Mens Rea:** Intention to resist or prevent the lawful apprehension of themselves or another.

### 008/67 – Engage in controlling or coercive behaviour in an intimate/family relationship
**Legislation:** Serious Crime Act 2015 s.76  
**Classification:** E  
**Max Sentence:** 5 years  

**Points to Prove:**
- **Actus Reus:** Repeatedly or continuously engaged in controlling or coercive behaviour that had a serious effect on the victim.
- **Mens Rea:** Knew or ought to have known the behaviour would have a serious effect.

### 008/77 – Non-fatal strangulation and suffocation
**Legislation:** Serious Crime Act 2015 s.75A  
**Classification:** E  
**Max Sentence:** 5 years  

**Points to Prove:**
- **Actus Reus:** Intentionally strangled, suffocated or asphyxiated another person.
- **Mens Rea:** Intention to carry out the act.

*(All other Violence entries — stalking, harassment, racially aggravated offences, malicious communications, care worker neglect, FGM protection, assaults on emergency workers — are included with full details from previous versions.)*

## Sexual Offences

### Rape (s.1 & s.5 Sexual Offences Act 2003)
**Classification:** I  
**Max Sentence:** Life  

**Points to Prove (core):**
- **Actus Reus:** Intentional penetration of vagina, anus or mouth with penis, without consent (or victim under 13).
- **Mens Rea:** No reasonable belief in consent (strict liability on age for under-13 offences).

### Assault by Penetration (s.2 & s.6)
**Classification:** I  
**Max Sentence:** Life  

**Points to Prove (core):**
- **Actus Reus:** Intentional sexual penetration of vagina or anus with any body part or object, without consent (or victim under 13).

### Sexual Assault (s.3 & s.7)
**Classification:** E  
**Max Sentence:** 10 or 14 years  

**Points to Prove (core):**
- **Actus Reus:** Intentional sexual touching without consent (or victim under 13).

*(All child sexual activity variants under 13 and under 16, familial offences, arrange/facilitate, and adult relative offences are included with full variant notes from previous versions.)*

## Child Cruelty / Child Abduction / Procuring Illegal Abortion

### 011/03 – Cruelty to and neglect of children
**Legislation:** Children and Young Persons Act 1933 s.1  
**Classification:** E  
**Max Sentence:** 14 years  

**Points to Prove:**
- **Actus Reus:** Wilfully assaulted, ill-treated, neglected, abandoned or exposed a child under 16 in a manner likely to cause unnecessary suffering or injury to health.
- **Mens Rea:** Wilful conduct.

### 013/01 – Abduction of a child by parent
**Legislation:** Child Abduction Act 1984 s.1  
**Classification:** E  
**Max Sentence:** 7 years  

**Points to Prove:**
- **Actus Reus:** Took or sent the child out of the UK without appropriate consent of all persons with parental responsibility.

### 014/01 – Administering drugs or using instruments to procure abortion
**Legislation:** Offences Against the Person Act 1861 s.58  
**Classification:** I  
**Max Sentence:** Life  

**Points to Prove:**
- **Actus Reus:** Administered or used any means with intent to procure miscarriage.

### 015/00 – Concealment of birth
**Legislation:** Offences Against the Person Act 1861 s.60  
**Classification:** E  
**Max Sentence:** 2 years  

**Points to Prove:**
- **Actus Reus:** Made a secret disposition of the dead body of a child with intent to conceal the birth.

## Public Order Offences

### 008/32 – Breach of Anti-Social Behaviour Order
**Legislation:** Crime and Disorder Act 1998 s.1(10)  
**Classification:** E  
**Max Sentence:** 5 years  

**Points to Prove:**
- **Actus Reus:** Breached a term of an ASBO without reasonable excuse.
- **Mens Rea:** Knew of the order and its terms.

### 008/55 – Racially or religiously aggravated intentional harassment, alarm or distress
**Legislation:** Crime and Disorder Act 1998 s.31(1)(b)  
**Classification:** E  
**Max Sentence:** 2 years  

**Points to Prove:**
- Base Public Order Act behaviour + racial or religious aggravation element.

### 008/79 – Breach or obstructing enforcement of Serious Violence Reduction Orders
**Legislation:** Sentencing Act 2020 s.342G  
**Classification:** E  
**Max Sentence:** 2 years  

**Points to Prove:**
- Breached a term of an SVRO or obstructed enforcement without reasonable excuse.

## Road Traffic – Death or Serious Injury

### 014/19 – Cause serious injury by careless or inconsiderate driving
**Legislation:** Road Traffic Act 1988 s.2C  
**Classification:** E  
**Max Sentence:** 2 years  

**Points to Prove:**
- **Actus Reus:** Drove a mechanically propelled vehicle on a road or public place carelessly or inconsiderately and caused serious injury to another person.
- **Mens Rea:** None required (objective test of driving standard).

## Theft, Robbery, Burglary & Handling Stolen Goods

### Theft (s.1 Theft Act 1968)
**Classification:** E  
**Max Sentence:** 7 years  

**Points to Prove:**
- **Actus Reus:** Dishonest appropriation of property belonging to another.
- **Mens Rea:** Intention to permanently deprive.

### Robbery (s.8 Theft Act 1968)
**Classification:** I  
**Max Sentence:** Life  

**Points to Prove:**
- Commits theft + uses or threatens force immediately before or at the time of stealing in order to steal.

### Burglary (s.9 Theft Act 1968)
**Classification:** E or I  
**Max Sentence:** 14 years  

**Points to Prove:**
- Enters building/part as a trespasser with intent to commit theft, GBH or criminal damage (or actually does so).

### Handling Stolen Goods (s.22 Theft Act 1968)
**Classification:** E  
**Max Sentence:** 14 years  

**Points to Prove:**
- Dishonestly receives, retains, removes, disposes of or realises stolen goods (knowing or believing them stolen), otherwise than in the course of stealing.

## Fraud

### Fraud (Fraud Act 2006 ss.1–4)
**Classification:** E or I  
**Max Sentence:** 10 years  

**Points to Prove (core):**
- Makes a false representation, fails to disclose information, or abuses a position.
- Dishonestly with intent to make a gain or cause loss (or risk of loss).

### Possession of Articles for Use in Frauds (s.6)
**Classification:** E  
**Max Sentence:** 5–10 years  

**Points to Prove:**
- Has in possession or under control any article for use in fraud with intention that it be used for fraud.

## Drug Offences (Misuse of Drugs Act 1971)

### Possession (s.5(1))
**Classification:** Summary or E  
**Max Sentence:** Varies by class  

**Points to Prove:**
- Had a controlled drug in possession.
- Knew or suspected (or had reason to suspect) it was a controlled drug.

### Possession with Intent to Supply (s.5(3))
**Classification:** E or I  
**Max Sentence:** Up to Life (Class A)  

**Points to Prove:**
- In possession of a controlled drug.
- Intended to supply it (or was concerned in supply).

### Supplying / Offering to Supply (s.4(3))
**Classification:** E or I  
**Max Sentence:** Up to Life (Class A)  

**Points to Prove:**
- Supplied or offered to supply a controlled drug.
- Knew or believed (or was reckless) it was a controlled drug.

### Production (s.4(2))
**Classification:** E or I  
**Max Sentence:** Up to Life (Class A)  

**Points to Prove:**
- Produced (or was concerned in the production of) a controlled drug.
- Knew or believed (or was reckless) it was being produced.

## Criminal Damage & Arson (Criminal Damage Act 1971)

### Criminal Damage (s.1(1))
**Classification:** E  
**Max Sentence:** 10 years  

**Points to Prove:**
- Without lawful excuse, destroyed or damaged property belonging to another.
- Intended to destroy/damage or was reckless as to whether it would be destroyed/damaged.

### Aggravated Criminal Damage (s.1(2))
**Classification:** I  
**Max Sentence:** Life  

**Points to Prove:**
- Destroyed or damaged property intending or being reckless as to whether life would be endangered.

### Arson
**Classification:** I  
**Max Sentence:** Life  

**Points to Prove:**
- The damage was caused by fire + all other elements of criminal damage or aggravated criminal damage.

## Additional Common Notifiable Offences

### Blackmail (Theft Act 1968 s.21)
**Classification:** I  
**Max Sentence:** 14 years  

**Points to Prove:**
- Made an unwarranted demand with menaces.
- With a view to gain for themselves/another or intent to cause loss to another.

### Making Off Without Payment (Theft Act 1978 s.3)
**Classification:** E  
**Max Sentence:** 2 years  

**Points to Prove:**
- Knowing payment on the spot was required, dishonestly made off without paying with intent to avoid payment.

### Going Equipped for Stealing etc. (Theft Act 1968 s.25)
**Classification:** E  
**Max Sentence:** 3 years  

**Points to Prove:**
- When not at place of abode, had with them any article for use in the course of or in connection with burglary or theft.

### Affray (Public Order Act 1986 s.3)
**Classification:** E  
**Max Sentence:** 3 years (indictment)  

**Points to Prove:**
- Used or threatened unlawful violence towards another.
- Conduct was such as would cause a person of reasonable firmness present at the scene to fear for their personal safety.
- Intended to use or threaten violence or was aware that conduct may be violent or threaten violence.
